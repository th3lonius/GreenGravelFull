Green Gravel Comedy Fest

Iowa Laugh Harder

Toledo, IA

About

The Green Gravel Comedy Festival is a three-day alternative comedy festival in multiple venues in the town of Toledo, Iowa. Offering affordable, accessible shows and workshops in alternative comedy, Green Gravel aims to bring the finest comedic voices to the heart of Iowa. Our fest is made possible by the community of Toledo in conjunction with sponsors, partnerships and volunteer contributions. It’s time. Because Iowa laughs harder.

We’re proud to bring an array of the finest stand-up, podcast, improv and sketch comedians to Toledo, and we don’t believe that patrons should have to pay an arm and a leg to do it. That’s why we’re offering affordable options for ticketing: you can pop in for a single show, buy a day pass or snap up the Green Graveller weekend pass. You won’t be getting comedy-clubbed to death by minimum-drink charges; furthermore, we’ll be offering drink/merch specials as a thank-you to the folks who make the gravel strip comedy trip!

Venues

The
Wieting
Theatre

The lovely Wieting Theater serves as the primary venue for Green Gravel. The Wieting has brought fine arts ranging from live opera to 3D movies to the Toledo region since 1912. This 300-seat balcony-tiered gem has been restored and maintained by the community, showcasing the tireless work and cultural heritage of the people of Toledo. We’re honored to host our event here and can’t wait to share this completely unique live, hilarious experience with you!

Bumblebee’s

Just down the block from The Wieting Theater, across from the Tama County courthouse, sits a local institution for gourmet pizza, quality karaoke and late-night spirits for locals and visitors alike. This first-generation pool hall has been the hitching post for many a weary worker for over 2 decades and is owned/operated with pride by the Musgrave family. Bumblebee’s will be hosting comedy events in conjunction with those at the Wieting and a third venue to be announced.

Born and raised in Sioux City, Lee is a comedy writer and producer living in Los Angeles. He received his MFA in Dramatic Writing with honors from the Savannah College of Art and Design. He has written for ScreenJunkies.com, The Devastator Quarterly, Crave Comedy Online and more; this work has turned up in places like The A.V. Club, The Huffington Post and /Film. Keeler produces and hosts The Hour of Power Hour, a monthly comedy variety event at the Improv Hollywood that regularly sells out. The show has consistently boasted some of the finest voices in comedy today, including TJ Miller, Kyle Kinane, David Spade, Greg Proops, Paul Scheer, Maria Bamford and many more.

Matthew hails from Savannah, Georgia but has made his home in Iowa City, Iowa as a web developer, writer, designer and pool junkie. He currently attends the University of Iowa pursuing a BFA in Graphic Design while working for Epiphany Design Co, a local web development startup. He has written film reviews for the local Iowa City monthly "Little Village" and he is also the founder, designer and curator of Cinelogue, an online film essay journal. He is a dedicated Atlanta Braves fan and occasionally performs pieces for 12 string guitar around town.

Ohio born Evan Watkins is a comedy content producer based in Los Angeles. He's quickly made a name for himself producing video content for The Laugh Factory comedy club's online markets. He's had a hand in creating various sketches, podcasts and stand-up clips, working alongside comedians such as Dom Irrera, Adam Ray, Finesse Mitchell and many more. As for live comedy, he wrote and produced a year-long run of a successful standup and sketch show, "Comedy Slice" at the Hollywood Improv club. He is currently a sketch comedy writer and performer at the Improv Olympic West Theater, an avid hockey fan, and podcaster. His Pittsburgh themed Yinzcast can be found on iTunes.
